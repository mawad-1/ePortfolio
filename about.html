About Me

My Professional Journey

I am currently pursuing a dual major in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Detroit Mercy, driven by a strong interest in how both hardware and software work together to create innovative technology. My passion for Computer Science began in high school, where I discovered how much I enjoy problem-solving and building solutions through code. As I continued my education, I became increasingly interested in expanding my knowledge into Electrical Engineering to better understand the systems behind the technology I develop.

Although I have recently completed my sophomore year and am still early in my academic journey, I have primarily completed foundational math and science coursework that has strengthened my analytical thinking, discipline, and ability to approach complex problems. My background in mathematics, in particular, has helped me develop a logical mindset that I apply when learning programming and tackling technical challenges. I am currently on track to graduate in 2029, and looking ahead, I see myself pursuing a career as an electrical engineer, where I can apply these skills to design and improve systems that solve real-world problems. I am excited to continue building the knowledge and experience needed to reach that goal.

Approach & Philosophy

When approaching difficult problems, I focus on breaking them down into smaller, manageable parts and working through them step by step. I value persistence and continuous learning, especially when faced with challenges that push me outside of my comfort zone. I also enjoy collaborating with others, as working in a team allows for new perspectives and more effective problem-solving.

  • Analytical Thinking: Strong ability to break down complex problems and approach them logically
  • Adaptability: Willingness to learn new concepts and adjust to challenging situations
  • Collaboration: Effective communication and teamwork in academic and group settings
